---
name: No artificial budgets on legitimate analysis or indexing work
description: NEVER cap the throughput/thoroughness of legitimate work with hardcoded search-call ceilings, agent-turn caps, or per-file/job timeouts — correctness over bounded cost
type: feedback
---

NEVER put an artificial budget/cap on a unit of legitimate analysis or indexing WORK. This includes: hardcoded "AT MOST N search calls" ceilings in Claude analysis prompts, agent-turn caps that can truncate a long-but-valid run, and per-file/per-job/whole-subprocess wall-clock timeouts. If the work is legitimate, let it take as many searches / turns / seconds as it genuinely needs.

**Why:** Capping a unit of legitimate work trades correctness for a false sense of bounded cost, and it silently degrades exactly the hardest cases (the biggest domains, the largest repos). This is the same anti-pattern the Bug #1218 timeout purge removed from the indexing path (no job/subprocess/per-file timeouts — a large repo legitimately takes hours). A concrete repeat offender: `dependency_map_analyzer.py` `_build_output_first_prompt` throttled LARGE domains to "AT MOST 5 search_code calls" and framed searching as optional ("write your analysis FIRST"), while the small-domain builder (`_build_std_mcp_search`) correctly told Claude searching is mandatory with a floor of "AT LEAST 3" and no ceiling. The cap was applied inversely to complexity — the domains needing the most searching got the least. The user has repeatedly said NOT to put budgets on legitimate work.

**How to apply:** When reviewing or writing any Claude-invoking analysis prompt or indexing/generation path, treat a hardcoded work cap as a defect: flag it and REMOVE it. Do NOT under-react to it as a "supporting detail" — it is a bug in its own right. On these jobs the user's stance is ABSOLUTE and he has repeated it angrily across multiple messages: remove EVERY numeric budget with NO exceptions — search-call ceilings ("AT MOST N calls"), agent-turn caps (set to 0 = unlimited), AND hard output-length ranges (e.g. "between 3,000 and 10,000 characters"). Do NOT carve out "borderline" exceptions, do NOT keep one because it "shapes output," and NEVER ask the user which caps to keep — "no budgets" means none. Purely non-numeric editorial guidance ("keep sections concise") may remain. The only bounds that survive anywhere are: (a) a single outbound provider HTTP request's connect/read timeout + its retry/backoff, and (b) a genuine runaway-loop backstop with provable termination (Messi Rule #14) — neither of which is a per-job/analysis work cap. See [[project_query_is_everything]] and CLAUDE.md "Indexing Path Has No Job/Subprocess/Per-File Timeouts (Bug #1218)".
